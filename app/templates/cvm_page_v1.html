About

CRESCENT Community Velocity Model (CVM)

CVM-Web is a web-based application for previewing, visualizing, and extracting data from the Community Velocity Model compiled by the Cascadia Region Earthquake Science Center. The CVM is a curated collection of seismic velocity models covering the Cascadia Subduction Zone and surrounding region.

The repository page lists the available 3D and 2D models with full metadata. Click a model to view its details and see its boundary on the 3D map. Use the filter controls to narrow the list by geographic area, depth, and parameters, or compare up to five models side by side.

Contribute a model (Jupyter notebook guide)

Tools

  • Cross-Section Viewer — vertical slices through a model along an arbitrary path.
  • Horizontal-Slice Viewer — constant-depth slices through a model.
  • Depth-Profile Viewer — vertical profiles at a single point.
  • Volume Data — download 3D subvolumes.
  • Cross-Section Data — download vertical-slice data.
  • Depth-Slice Data — download horizontal-slice data.

Map Features

  • CRESCENT Study Area outlined in cyan.
  • 3D Model Outline of the selected model shown in cyan.
  • Selected Model Extent shown in blue, adjustable via lat / lon / depth sliders and an opacity control.
  • Earthquakes (yellow dots) are events of magnitude greater than 4 within the CRESCENT study area since 1970, sized by magnitude. Sourced from the ANSS Comprehensive Earthquake Catalog via the USGS FDSN Event Service.
  • Imagery Layer toggle adds Cesium imagery for context; Terrain Occlusion unhides data when imagery is opaque.
  • Feature Toggles control visibility of fault traces, earthquakes, and other elements.
  • Center View recenters on the CRESCENT study area; Home View resets to the default perspective.
  • Measure Distance lets you click two points to measure their separation in kilometers.
